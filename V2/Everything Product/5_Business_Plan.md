## üå™Ô∏è SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
*   **First Mover (AI Agent Network)**: Multi-agent system (`CaseAnalyzer`, `ProcedureGuide`) is superior to single-prompt wrappers.
*   **UX/UI**: "Apple-like" design in an industry used to Windows 95 aesthetics.

### Weaknesses
*   **Data Moat**: We rely on public data (IndianKanoon). Need to build proprietary datasets over time.
*   **Trust Barrier**: Old-school senior advocates are skeptical of AI.

### Opportunities
*   **Regional Languages**: 60% of district court litigation happens in Hindi/Tamil/Marathi. Adding vernacular support opens a huge market.
*   **B2B Enterprise**: White-labeling the engine for big firms to search their *internal* archives.

### Threats
*   **Regulatory Risk**: Bar Council of India (BCI) regulations on "Artificial Lawyers". (Mitigation: We brand strictly as an *Assistant*, not a Lawyer).

## üõ°Ô∏è Risk Mitigation Strategy

### 1. The "Hallucination" Risk
*   **Risk**: AI cites a fake case. Lawyer uses it in court. Disaster.
*   **Mitigation**: **"The Citation Guarantee"**.
    *   Every case cited MUST have a real link.
    *   UI Warning: "Verify all citations with original text."

### 2. The "Data Privacy" Risk
*   **Risk**: Client data leaks.
*   **Mitigation**:
    *   **Local Storage**: Browser-based encryption for sensitive drafts.
    *   **Zero-Retention Policy**: We don't store user queries for training unless opted-in.
    *   **SOC-2 Compliance** (Year 2 goal).
